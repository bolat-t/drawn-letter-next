Direction 1: Social Communication

Concept: The "anti-Instagram"—no filters, no photos, just raw hand-drawn messages between friends.

  • Market gap exists (BeReal acquired for €500M validates authentic social)
  • Strong viral mechanics (friend-to-friend sharing)
  • Network effects create defensible moat

Direction 2: Gaming/Entertainment

Concept: Daily timed challenges and global drawing battles (Wordle meets Pictionary).

  • Draw Something discontinued in 2022—gap in market
  • High engagement, daily return behavior
  • Multiple monetization options (cosmetics, tournaments)

Direction 3: Wellness/Reflection

Concept: Mindful journaling through physical drawing movement—process emotions where words fail.

  • Mental health app market: $7.5B and growing 15-20% annually
  • Art therapy has clinical validation
  • B2B potential (therapists, corporate wellness)

Direction 4: Gifting/Cards

Concept: Draw a card on your phone, we print and mail a real postcard to their door.

  • E-cards market: $0.23B growing to $0.63B by 2033
  • High ARPU ($30-60 per active user)
  • Physical print margins 60%+

Direction 5: Creative/Art

Concept: A drawing studio for people who think they "can't draw"—expression over technique.

  • Digital illustration market: $8.9B
  • Challenge: Procreate dominates, hard to compete on features
  • Not recommended as primary direction

03 Recommended Strategy: Hybrid Approach

The Formula: Social (growth engine) + Wellness (meaning & value) + Gaming (daily habit)

This hybrid approach works because:

  • No competitor combines all three—creates unique positioning
  • Multiple engagement hooks—users come for different reasons
  • Viral + valuable—social provides growth, wellness provides stickiness
  • Revenue diversification—subscriptions + transactions + B2B

07 Go-to-Market Plan

90-Day Launch Plan

Weeks 1-4: Foundation

  • Private beta with 100 hand-selected users
  • Implement viral referral mechanism
  • Optimize onboarding flow based on feedback
  • Gather qualitative feedback

Weeks 5-8: Soft Launch

  • Expand to 1,000 users via waitlist
  • Launch college ambassador program (5 campuses)
  • Begin TikTok content strategy
  • Submit for App Store featuring

Weeks 9-12: Public Launch

  • Remove waitlist, open to all
  • PR push to wellness/tech media
  • Influencer partnerships (5 micro-influencers)

10 Conclusion & Next Steps

Bottom Line: Drawn Letter sits at the intersection of three growing trends—anti-Instagram authenticity, creative wellness, and casual gaming. The hand-gesture AI is a unique, defensible advantage. The hybrid strategy captures multiple audiences while standing out from all competitors.

Immediate Next Steps

  1. Week 1-2: Implement friend connections and drawing sharing
  2. Week 3-4: Add daily challenge mechanic
  3. Week 5-6: Launch private beta with 100 users
  4. Week 7-12: Iterate based on feedback, prepare for public launch

Key Decisions Needed

  • Primary platform focus: iOS-first or cross-platform?
  • Print partner selection for physical postcards
  • Content pipeline: In-house prompts or community-generated?
  • Team structure: Engineering vs. marketing investment split
